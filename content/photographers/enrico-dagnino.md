    caption: "Sarajevo, Bosnia. 93/94The airport was officially controlled by UNPROFOR. It was used by military planes with humanitarian aid for the city, foreign journalists, artists, politicians, mediators, diplomats and the citizens of Sarajevo with UN Press or UNHCR cards. The airport was opened or closed depending on the Serbs shelling or their guarantees of safety in accordance with their current political or military aims. In reality the Serbs controlled the airport.The role of the UNPROFOR was manifold. They served as hostages to the Serbs, they cleared the garbage, they rode in APC as a protection against snipers, they gave out sweets, brought flour, destroyed the surface of Sarajevo streets with their tanks and transporters, representing the only city transportation through a long period, they repaired electrical transmissions lines, they controlled the airport ... The most important form of the protection of citizens was driving the APC next to them shielding them from the sniper fire while they were crossing the Tito street. For a long time they were the most significant part of the city's commercial life because they were trading the goods available to them."
